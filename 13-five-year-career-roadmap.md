# SECTION 13 — Five-Year Career Roadmap

**Anchor date:** July 2026  
**North star:** Internationally registered physiotherapist practising safely in a high-quality health system (UK/AU/NZ/CA or Germany), with a developing specialty identity and sustainable finances.

---

## YEAR 1 (Jul 2026 – Jun 2027) — Foundation & Unlock

### Clinical
- Complete / extend high-quality supervised practice at MTH; request detailed duty letters  
- Start weekly competency log (assessment, treatment, outcomes, reflection)  
- Deliver 3 anonymised case studies for portfolio  
- Obtain BLS/CPR; infection control / moving-handling where available  

### Academic
- Confirm research citation or convert to “final-year project” language  
- Begin literature habit: 2 papers/month in MSK or neuro  
- Draft clinical audit proposal (documentation quality or HEP adherence)

### Professional
- Maintain PPTA/World Physiotherapy membership  
- Finalise LinkedIn + portfolio website  
- Build Section 9 document archive  

### International migration
- Sit **IELTS/OET**  
- Choose primary country (recommended decision point: UK vs NZ)  
- Request curriculum + clinical hours from university  
- Submit **one** primary registration pathway application when pack ready  

### Language
- English test success  
- If Germany Tier-1: German A1–A2  

### Financial
- Build registration fund: target savings covering English test + primary regulator fees + document legalisation  
- Avoid paying multiple full pathways simultaneously  

### Leadership
- Continue mentoring juniors informally; collect teaching feedback note if possible  

### Specialisation signal
- Choose provisional track: **MSK** or **Neuro** (not both as “specialist” yet)

**Year 1 success metrics:** English pass; dossier complete; BLS current; primary application submitted; public brand live.

---

## YEAR 2 (Jul 2027 – Jun 2028) — Registration & First International Role

### Clinical
- If registered: secure graduate/junior supervised role (e.g., NHS Band 5 equivalent / private hospital junior / community rehab)  
- If not yet registered: continue supervised practice + exam/adaptation prep  
- Complete first formal clinical audit cycle  

### Academic
- Present audit/poster at a national or online conference  
- Short course: manual therapy or neurological rehab (country-recognised)

### Professional
- Join relevant association (CSP associate/full when eligible; or AU/NZ/CA equivalent)  
- Structured CPD log meeting local regulator hours  

### International migration
- Complete registration (HCPC / PBNZ / Ahpra / CAPR exam progress / German compensation measure)  
- Visa application with regulated adviser support  
- Relocate if approved  

### Financial
- Transition from savings drawdown to employed income  
- Keep 3-month emergency fund in destination currency when possible  

### Leadership
- Supervise students if role allows; join quality-improvement mini-team  

### Specialisation
- Confirm specialty direction with caseload ≥60% alignment  

**Year 2 success metrics:** Licence or clear late-stage progress; first international employment or adaptation placement; audit completed.

---

## YEAR 3 (Jul 2028 – Jun 2029) — Consolidation & Competence Depth

### Clinical
- Manage fuller junior caseload with decreasing supervision needs  
- Expand into second setting (e.g., outpatients + wards, or community)  
- Outcome-measure dashboard for personal practice (ethical, anonymised)

### Academic
- Consider postgraduate certificate / master’s exploratory applications (neuro, MSK, rehab science, public health)  
- Co-author case report or QI publication  

### Professional
- Stable registration renewal + good standing  
- Mentor one internationally trained peer (pay-it-forward brand)

### International migration
- If NZ registered: evaluate AU TTMR  
- If UK registered: evaluate long-term settlement / further specialist pathways  
- If Germany: complete recognition and language to confident clinical B2/C1  

### Financial
- Debt control from migration costs; begin retirement/savings habit  
- Fund one major CPD course without credit stress  

### Leadership
- Lead a small service improvement project  
- Present in-service training to team  

### Specialisation
- Specialty short fellowships / hospital rotations if available  

**Year 3 success metrics:** Solid junior competence reputation; specialty clarity; financial stabilisation.

---

## YEAR 4 (Jul 2029 – Jun 2030) — Specialty Acceleration

### Clinical
- Seek roles in neurological centre, orthopaedic hospital, sports clinic, or advanced MSK outpatients aligned to specialty  
- Develop niche skills (vestibular intro, pain science, sports rehab, neurodevelopmental—choose one coherent path)

### Academic
- Enrol in master’s **or** structured advanced practice modules if ROI clear  
- Conference presentation outside home institution  

### Professional
- Build referral reputation inside organisation  
- Maintain spotless documentation and complaint-free practice  

### International migration
- Second-country mobility only if strategically valuable (e.g., NZ→AU)  
- Avoid serial exam tourism that stalls clinical depth  

### Financial
- Income growth via specialty role / additional clinic sessions where permitted  
- Fund master’s partially via employer support if available  

### Leadership
- Supervise juniors formally; contribute to induction programmes  

### Specialisation
- Portfolio evidence for advanced practice later (not claiming title early)

**Year 4 success metrics:** Recognised as reliable specialty-aligned clinician; postgraduate study underway or completed modules.

---

## YEAR 5 (Jul 2030 – Jun 2031) — Leadership & International Optionality

### Clinical
- Senior junior / mid-level competence depending on system  
- Complex case ownership within scope  

### Academic
- Complete master’s **or** equivalent advanced credentials  
- Publish QI or clinical paper  

### Professional
- Consider teaching affiliate / clinical educator role  
- Evaluate fellowship pathways (neuro/MSK/sports)

### International migration
- Hold portable registration + clean good-standing history  
- Optionality across target countries without restarting identity from zero  

### Financial
- Stable professional income; migration costs repaid; investment habit active  

### Leadership
- Lead audit programme or student education stream  
- External networking: World Physiotherapy congress / national conference  

### Specialisation
- Clear public specialty narrative backed by caseload + CPD + outcomes  

**Year 5 success metrics:** Mid-career trajectory established; teaching/leadership footprint; internationally portable professional identity.
