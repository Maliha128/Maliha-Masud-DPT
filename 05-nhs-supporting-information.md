## Supporting Information

I am applying for this physiotherapy role because I want to practise within a system that places patient safety, dignity and evidence-based rehabilitation at the centre of care. I am a Doctor of Physiotherapy graduate from The University of Faisalabad (CGPA 3.58/4.00) and currently work as a Clinical Physiotherapist at Madina Teaching Hospital, a teaching-hospital environment where I evaluate neuromuscular/MSK conditions, manage tailored rehabilitation protocols, and maintain documentation within multidisciplinary collaboration. I am preparing my pathway to HCPC registration and appropriate English language evidence, and I am committed to meeting UK professional standards before using the protected title of Physiotherapist in independent practice.

### Compassion

In my internship I regularly support patients recovering from musculoskeletal injury, neurological impairment and post-operative procedures. Many patients arrive anxious, in pain, or uncertain about recovery. I focus first on listening carefully, explaining findings in accessible language, and agreeing realistic short-term goals. For example, when educating patients on home exercise programmes, I check understanding, adapt instructions to the patient’s physical and cognitive capacity, and involve caregivers where appropriate. Compassion, for me, is not sentiment; it is consistent attention to comfort, dignity, consent and emotional safety during every assessment and treatment session.

### Respect and Dignity

I treat each patient as a partner in rehabilitation. I obtain consent before hands-on assessment or modality use, protect privacy during examination, and remain mindful of cultural and personal boundaries. Working in a busy teaching hospital has reinforced that respectful practice also means punctuality, clear communication, and never dismissing a patient’s report of pain or fear. I apply the same respect to colleagues—recognising the contribution of nurses, physicians and other allied professionals to safe outcomes.

### Improving Lives / Commitment to Quality of Care

My clinical focus is restoring function: mobility, strength, confidence and participation in daily activities. Across musculoskeletal, neurological and post-operative pathways I contribute to rehabilitation plans that combine therapeutic exercise, manual therapy techniques and modality-supported care where indicated (including ultrasound, TENS, heat and cold therapy), always under supervision and within my competence. I monitor response to intervention and escalate concerns promptly. Quality of care also means knowing the limits of my experience as an early-career clinician and seeking guidance rather than guessing.

### Working Together for Patients (Teamwork)

Rehabilitation outcomes depend on coordinated care. At Madina Teaching Hospital I collaborate with doctors, nurses and the wider team to align physiotherapy goals with medical plans and discharge needs. I communicate assessment findings clearly, document progress accurately, and adjust plans when clinical status changes. Between October 2025 and May 2026 I also supported clinical teaching in the physiotherapy outpatient department, helping students and junior interns understand bedside assessment protocols. That experience strengthened my ability to explain clinical reasoning and to model professional behaviour for others.

### Everyone Counts / Inclusion

I am motivated to work in the NHS because it serves people from every background. As a Pakistani physiotherapist who communicates in Urdu and English (currently B2, with planned formal English testing for HCPC), I understand how language, culture and health literacy affect engagement with care. I am committed to equitable treatment, non-discriminatory practice, and adapting education so that patients can participate meaningfully in their rehabilitation, regardless of age, disability, culture or communication needs.

### Communication

Effective physiotherapy depends on communication that is clinically precise and humanly clear. I explain assessment findings, treatment rationale and home programmes in plain language, confirm understanding, and document encounters so that colleagues can continue care safely. In teaching-support work I practised breaking down assessment steps for learners without compromising patient dignity. I welcome feedback and use it to refine both clinical and interpersonal performance.

### Patient Safety and Clinical Governance

Patient safety is non-negotiable. I practise within my competence, follow departmental protocols, and maintain accurate records that support continuity and accountability. I am attentive to contraindications for electrotherapy and manual techniques, infection-control expectations, and safe patient handling. I understand that NHS clinical governance requires openness about incidents, learning from error, and participation in audit and quality improvement as my career progresses. I am prepared to complete local mandatory training, follow Trust policies, and escalate risk immediately.

### Evidence-Based Practice

My academic training and clinical internship are grounded in evidence-informed decision-making. I select interventions based on assessment findings, current clinical guidance as available in my setting, and patient preference. I have completed targeted continuing development, including workshops on neurological assessment (stroke and cerebral palsy), spinal mobilisation concepts (Kaltenborn, Maitland and Mulligan frameworks), and a dry-needling techniques workshop, which I treat as foundational learning rather than a claim of independent specialist status. I also completed an online course related to low back pain and pelvic floor dysfunction. My research interest in myofascial trigger points in subacute hemiplegic shoulder presentations reflects a desire to connect clinical observation with enquiry; I will provide full publication details where required.

### Professional Integrity and Development

I am a member of the Pakistan Physical Therapy Association (membership number P14418, valid to 31 December 2029) and thereby linked to World Physiotherapy. I take professional membership seriously as a signal of ethical accountability. I am realistic about my career stage: I am an early-career clinician building towards international registration. What I offer immediately is reliability, supervised clinical competence across core pathways, strong documentation habits, teaching-support experience, and a values-aligned approach to patient care. What I commit to developing is HCPC registration, formal English evidence, NHS-specific systems familiarity, and continuous CPD.

I would welcome the opportunity to contribute to your team, learn within NHS structures, and deliver physiotherapy that is safe, compassionate and clinically effective.
