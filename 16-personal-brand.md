## Professional Biography — 500 words

Maliha Masud is an early-career physiotherapist building a carefully documented international professional identity grounded in verified training and supervised clinical practice. She completed her Doctor of Physiotherapy (DPT) at The University of Faisalabad between 2019 and 2024, graduating with a CGPA of 3.58/4.00 after a pre-medical higher secondary education at Punjab Group of Colleges and secondary education at Kindergarten Muslim Girls High School.

Since 18 December 2024, Maliha has served as a Physiotherapy House Officer at Madina Teaching Hospital, a teaching-hospital environment in Faisalabad. In this role she contributes to supervised assessment and rehabilitation planning for patients across musculoskeletal, neurological and post-operative pathways. Her clinical work includes therapeutic exercise prescription, manual therapy techniques within competence, and the application of physical agents such as ultrasound therapy, TENS, heat therapy and cold therapy when clinically indicated. She places particular importance on patient and caregiver education, home exercise planning, progress monitoring and accurate clinical documentation—habits that translate directly to the governance expectations of international employers.

From October 2025 to May 2026, Maliha also supported clinical teaching in the physiotherapy outpatient department at Madina Teaching Hospital. This included instructional guidance, bedside assessment protocol support and procedural demonstrations for students and junior interns. The experience reinforced her commitment to clear clinical communication and professional modelling.

Maliha is a member of the Pakistan Physical Therapy Association (membership number P14418; valid through 31 December 2029) and, through that national membership, of World Physiotherapy. Her continuing professional development includes a university dry-needling techniques workshop, a neurological assessment workshop focused on stroke and cerebral palsy, spinal mobilisation concept training drawing on Kaltenborn, Maitland and Mulligan frameworks, and online learning related to low back pain and pelvic floor dysfunction. She represents these activities accurately as learning exposures rather than specialist certifications.

Her research interest centres on the prevalence of myofascial trigger points in the unaffected shoulder of subacute hemiplegic patients and associations with muscle tone, a topic listed in association with the Pakistan Armed Forces Medical Journal on prior application materials; full citation details are confirmed before public academic claims.

Beyond clinic, Maliha has participated in university-linked medical and public-health camps and maintains creative disciplines—reading, writing and visual arts—that support reflection and communication. She is fluent in Urdu and currently operates in English at a B2 level while preparing formal scores required for overseas registration.

Maliha’s international strategy is deliberate: assemble a complete credential dossier, obtain regulator-accepted English evidence, pursue lawful registration (HCPC, Ahpra/APC, Physiotherapy Board of New Zealand, CAPR, or German Anerkennung as applicable), and enter practice as a safe, supervised, values-aligned clinician. She does not claim a licence she does not hold. Her brand promise is simpler and stronger: rigorous training, honest representation, and readiness to meet the standards of the health system she seeks to join.
