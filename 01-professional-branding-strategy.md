## 4. Compensation Strategy (how to neutralise each weakness)

### 4.1 Early-career status
- Lead with **competency language**, not years.
- Front-load teaching-hospital setting, MDT work, clinical teaching, research orientation.
- Target **Band 5 / graduate / junior / new-graduate** pathways honestly; avoid senior titles.
- Build a **portfolio of anonymised case frameworks** (assessment → plan → outcome language without fabricated metrics).

### 4.2 Registration gap
- Publish a clear **registration roadmap** (Section 11) in LinkedIn About and website.
- Sequence strategically: **English test → HCPC or NZ General Pathway → then AU TTMR if NZ secured**, or direct APEP for Australia; CAPR for Canada; German Anerkennung + B2/C1 German.
- Never apply for clinical posts requiring active registration until eligible; apply for **supervised / assistant / support** roles only where legally appropriate.

### 4.3 English
- Immediate priority: **IELTS Academic overall 7.0 (no band < 6.5)** or **OET** equivalent for HCPC.
- Until then, keep English listed as B2 and state “IELTS/OET preparation in progress” only if true.

### 4.4 German
- If Germany remains a Tier-1 target: enrol in structured **German to B2 (healthcare vocabulary)**; Anerkennung authorities typically expect German for patient-facing practice.
- Prepare diploma recognition dossier early (degree, transcript, syllabus/curriculum, clinical hours profile).

### 4.5 Measurable language without invention
Use **process metrics**, not fake outcomes:
- “Completed supervised assessments across MSK, neurological and post-operative caseloads”
- “Maintained clinical documentation and progress notes to hospital standards”
- “Delivered structured patient education for home exercise adherence”
- “Supported clinical instruction for junior interns and students in OPD”

### 4.6 Workshop honesty
Phrase as:
- “Participated in … workshop”
- “Foundational exposure to …”
Never: “Certified dry needling practitioner” unless a recognised certification body certificate exists.

### 4.7 Research integrity
Obtain full citation. If published: list journal, year, volume, pages, DOI. If thesis/project only: label as **final-year research project**.

### 4.8 International title discipline
Standardise all five-country materials to **Maliha Masud, PT, DPT**.

## 7. Employer Perception Goal (30-second read)

A recruiter should conclude:

1. She is a **real clinician**, not only a graduate.
2. She understands **international licensing** and is preparing correctly.
3. She communicates in **safe, ethical, evidence-based language**.
4. She will not create title, scope, or documentation risk.
5. She is worth interviewing for early-career / supervised pathways.

---

## 8. Brand Rules (non-negotiable)

1. Never invent caseload numbers, grades beyond 3.58/4, or credentials.
2. Never claim independent practice rights abroad.
3. Never upgrade workshop participation to specialist certification.
4. Always separate **internship** from **permanent employment**.
5. Always pair ambition with a **registration + language plan**.
6. Every public sentence must increase employability or trust—never vanity.
